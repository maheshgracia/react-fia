f:T980,<p dir="ltr"><strong>GOP AG candidate vows to investigate every state shelter, targeting crime and lack of nonprofit accountability</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">Republican candidate for New York State Attorney General Saritha Komatireddy has unveiled a hardline strategy to address the state's escalating homelessness crisis. In a pointed campaign announcement, Komatireddy committed to launching a comprehensive audit and investigation into every homeless shelter across New York on her first day in office, citing a catastrophic failure in current oversight.</p>
<p dir="ltr">The Republican hopeful took direct aim at sitting Attorney General Letitia James, alleging a "dereliction of duty." Komatireddy argued that while the AG&rsquo;s Charities Bureau is tasked with regulating the nonprofits that run these facilities, the current administration has allowed them to operate without sufficient accountability. "We are spending millions in taxpayer dollars on homeless services each year, and yet we have more homeless New Yorkers," she noted, echoing concerns previously raised by city and state comptrollers.</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Komatireddy Pushes Shelter Reform Amid Safety Concerns</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">Komatireddy&rsquo;s platform characterizes existing shelters as "hubs for drugs and crime" rather than safe havens for the vulnerable. Her plan is built on a three-pronged approach: ensuring physical safety for residents, prosecuting criminal activity within and around shelter perimeters, and strictly measuring success through permanent housing and employment transitions.</p>
<p dir="ltr">The announcement coincides with a broader Republican push for land-use reform and shelter transparency. It follows recent protests led by gubernatorial candidate Bruce Blakeman against a proposed 150-bed men&rsquo;s shelter in Bensonhurst, where residents expressed fears over safety in residential corridors.</p>
<p dir="ltr">"Homeless Americans are dying on the streets of New York more than 20 this year alone," Komatireddy stated. "The Attorney General has a unique ability to take on this problem. I have the will to ensure these resources actually help New Yorkers transition back to a normal life." By focusing on the AG&rsquo;s role as a primary regulator, Komatireddy aims to shift the office&rsquo;s focus toward aggressive fiscal and safety audits of the state's massive shelter network.</p>
