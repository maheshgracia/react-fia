50 Indian Americans Among Forbes' 2026 30 Under 30 Changemakers

A Record-Breaking Generation of Innovators

At least 50 Indian Americans have secured spots in Forbes' prestigious 30 Under 30 List for 2026, marking one of the strongest showings yet for this community. The recognition comes as part of Forbes' selection of 600 transformative leaders across North America spanning 20 different industries, from artificial intelligence to social impact.

Gen Z Takes the Lead

This year's cohort represents a generational shift, with 70 percent of honorees belonging to Gen Z, a significant jump from 50 percent last year. Collectively, the Class of 2026 has raised $3.8 billion in funding and commands over 200 million followers on social media, demonstrating unprecedented reach and influence.

AI Innovation at the Forefront

Indian American entrepreneurs are dominating the artificial intelligence sector. Adit Abraham and Raunak Chowdhuri of Reducto have scaled their AI document intelligence platform to process nearly a billion pages monthly, securing over $100 million in funding at a $600 million valuation. Meanwhile, Karun Kaushik's Delve serves over 1,000 companies and recently raised $32 million in Series A funding for compliance automation.

Financial Technology and Healthcare Breakthroughs

The finance sector showcases impressive talent with leaders like Stanford alumni Ashi Agrawal and Kalyani Ramadurgam, who founded Kobalt Labs to automate risk and compliance operations for financial institutions. In healthcare, innovators like Arya Rao are revolutionizing medical education through AI-powered patient simulations at Massachusetts General Hospital's MESH Incubator.

Social Impact and Sustainability

Beyond profit-driven ventures, many honorees are addressing critical social challenges. Sarika Bajaj and Tushita Gupta's Refiberd uses AI technology to sort textiles for recycling with exceptional accuracy, attracting partnerships with eBay and GANNI while raising $4.7 million in funding. Amrita Bhasin's Sotira has diverted two million pounds of unsold inventory from landfills through innovative reuse platforms.

Education and Aerospace Leadership

The education sector features trailblazers like Anahita Dalmia, whose media-literacy game has reached over 120,000 students, and Rajya Atluri's Coral AI, serving 630,000 users across 190 countries. In aerospace, Ashika Gopalkrishnan redesigned NASA's funding systems, cutting processing time by 92 percent while saving $2 million annually.

This diverse cohort of Indian American changemakers demonstrates the community's expanding influence across every sector of the American economy.
