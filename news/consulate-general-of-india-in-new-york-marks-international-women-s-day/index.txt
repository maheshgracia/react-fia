<p>The&nbsp;Consulate General of India in New York, in collaboration with the&nbsp;Society of Foreign Consuls in New York, hosted a special event to commemorate&nbsp;International Women's Day, bringing together diplomats, public officials, and community leaders to celebrate the achievements and leadership of women across multiple sectors.</p>
<p>The gathering highlighted the important role women play in diplomacy, governance, and community development, while also recognizing their contributions to building stronger international partnerships and more inclusive societies. The event served as a platform to acknowledge women who have made significant strides in public service and international engagement.</p>
<p>Among the distinguished guests was&nbsp;Khatuna Totladze, whose presence underscored the global dimension of the celebration. Also in attendance were&nbsp;Aissata Camara&nbsp;and&nbsp;Ana Mar&iacute;a Archila, both of whom have played key roles in strengthening international and multicultural engagement in New York City.</p>
<p>A highlight of the evening was the recognition of several distinguished women leaders from around the world. Among them was Indian-American public official&nbsp;Charuni Patibanda-Sanchez, who was honored for her contributions to public service and her leadership in advancing civic engagement within the community.</p>
<p>In addition to the formal recognition ceremony, the program featured a series of cultural performances that reflected the diversity and vibrancy of the international community represented in New York. The performances added a festive dimension to the evening while celebrating cultural exchange and unity.</p>
