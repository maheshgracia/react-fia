<p dir="ltr">Saritha Komatireddy, a candidate for New York Attorney General and former federal prosecutor, has issued a sharp critique of the Humane Alternatives to Long-Term Solitary Confinement (HALT) Act, linking the legislation to a drastic decline in prison safety.&nbsp;</p>

<p dir="ltr">Komatireddy slammed the approximately $1.2 billion in taxpayer funds allocated for this "temporary fix," suggesting the capital would be better spent on infrastructure and expanding the workforce.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Citing recent homicides in facilities like Malone and Gouverneur, Komatireddy echoed warnings from the state&rsquo;s correctional officers' union regarding a surge in violence and contraband.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">She pledged to prioritize "law and order" within the correctional system, advocating for legislative amendments that would restore essential disciplinary tools to frontline officers and stabilize a system she describes as being under extreme pressure.</p>
