Indian-American Trailblazer Dr. Bobby Mukkamala Ignites AAPI-QLI's Milestone Gathering

AMA President Delivers Visionary Keynote at 29th Annual Convention

Dr. Bobby Mukkamala, the first Indian-origin president of the American Medical Association (AMA), captivated physicians at the American Association of Physicians of Indian Origin Queens-Long Island chapter's (AAPI-QLI) 29th Annual Convention on December 6, 2025, at the Hilton Long Island-Whitman, New York. As the largest AAPI chapter in North America, AAPI-QLI marked this occasion with a gala featuring comprehensive Continuing Medical Education (CME) programs, fostering professional growth and community bonds among Indian-American doctors.
Dr. Mukkamala's keynote emphasized unity, equity, and bold reforms in U.S. healthcare, drawing from his journey as an otolaryngologist from Flint, Michigan, and a brain cancer survivor. He urged physicians to advocate for compassionate, sustainable systems that honor patient dignity and professional resilience, inspiring attendees to lead transformative change. The event celebrated distinguished medical leaders, reinforcing the vital role of Indian-American professionals in elevating American medicine.
This convention highlights the remarkable achievements of the Indian diaspora in healthcare, where leaders like Dr. Mukkamala bridge cultures to drive progress. AAPI-QLI's success underscores growing influence, with engaging sessions on leadership and innovation that empower the community. Such gatherings affirm pride in shared heritage while advancing excellence in the U.S
