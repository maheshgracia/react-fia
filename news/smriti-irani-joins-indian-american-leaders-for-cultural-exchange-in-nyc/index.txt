f:T525,<p dir="ltr"><em>Former Union Minister hails diaspora's role in diplomacy and roots at NYC's Bharatiya Vidya Bhavan cultural gathering with music and unity.</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">Prominent Indian American community leaders gathered at New York's Bharatiya Vidya Bhavan this week for a high-profile meet-and-greet with former Union Minister of India Smriti Irani. The event was hosted at the Romanti Auditorium and celebrated diaspora contributions while strengthening India&ndash;U.S. cultural ties through dialogue and music.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Organized by Dr. Navin Mehta and Dr. H. R. Shah of TV Asia, the evening opened with a rare Hansaveena performance by Ramanan Venkatraman, accompanied on tabla by Aditya Phatak, and curated by the Sneh Arts Foundation reflecting India's deep artistic heritage. The gathering brought together heads of major organizations and cultural advocates to foster community unity.</p>
<p dir="ltr">In her address, Irani praised Bharatiya Vidya Bhavan and media outlets like TV Asia for anchoring the diaspora to their roots. She highlighted the growing influence of Indian Americans in diplomacy and global service. The event concluded as a powerful showcase of the community's leadership and its enduring connection to India's cultural evolution.</p>
