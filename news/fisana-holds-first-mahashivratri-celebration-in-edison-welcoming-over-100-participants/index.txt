f:T6c4,<p data-start="273" data-end="354"><em>Community Gathers for Inaugural Spiritual Event Marking a Key Cultural Moment</em></p>
<p data-start="356" data-end="673">The Federation of Indian American Associations of North America (FISANA) successfully hosted its&nbsp;first-ever Mahashivratri puja, bringing together more than 100 members of the Indian American community for a meaningful evening of prayer and cultural observance.</p>
<p data-start="675" data-end="1027">The celebration took place at the Golden Era Day Care Center in Edison, where participants gathered for approximately two hours to honor Mahashivratri, a major night of devotion in the Hindu tradition dedicated to Lord Shiva. The event provided a chance for reflection, devotion, and community bonding.</p>
<p data-start="1029" data-end="1441">Organized under the leadership of FISANA President Sureshbhai Patel and Secretary Yogesh Trivedi, the program was described by organizers as a landmark initiative designed to deepen cultural and spiritual ties. Volunteers, sponsors, and committee members supported the evening, contributing to a seamless experience for attendees.</p>
<p data-start="1443" data-end="1811">The venue was generously provided by Bhagwat Parmar, owner of the Golden Era facility, who offered the space for worship without charge. Leaders with FISANA noted that the strong turnout and peaceful atmosphere highlighted a growing interest in cultural and spiritual programming among Indian Americans in the New Jersey area.</p>
