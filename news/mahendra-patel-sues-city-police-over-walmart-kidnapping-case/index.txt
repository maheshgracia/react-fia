f:T752,<p><strong><em>Georgia Engineer Seeks Justice After False Arrest Upends Life<br></em></strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">Mahendra Patel, a 57-year-old engineer from Acworth, Georgia, has filed a federal civil rights lawsuit in Atlanta's U.S. District Court, targeting the mother who accused him of attempted kidnapping, local police, and Cobb County prosecutors. The action, lodged on February 9, follows the dismissal of all charges last August after Walmart surveillance footage exonerated him, revealing a brief, innocent interaction while he shopped for Tylenol.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">Patel endured 47 harrowing days in Cobb County Jail without bond, facing threats and isolation that shattered his family and business. He alleges authorities ignored clear video evidence showing no force or intent to harm, pressing felony counts of kidnapping attempt, assault, and battery despite exculpatory proof. Named defendants include accuser Caroline Miller, District Attorney Sonya Allen, the city of Acworth, a lead detective, and a DA investigator.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">His legal team at Milberg demands compensatory and punitive damages for lost wages, emotional trauma, and reputational harm, underscoring a critical need for accountability in rushed prosecutions. Patel, who rebuilt his life post prior incarceration, calls the episode a nightmare but stands resolute: "A simple trip to Walmart turned into the biggest headache of my life." As this case unfolds, it spotlights protections for everyday citizens caught in investigative misfires, affirming the justice system's capacity for correction.</p>
