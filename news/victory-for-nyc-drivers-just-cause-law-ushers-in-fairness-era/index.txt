<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">New York City Council Member Shekar Krishnan, alongside the New York Taxi Workers Alliance (NYTWA), celebrated a historic win, with the passage of Intro 276. This landmark bill ends arbitrary "deactivations" for nearly 100,000 Uber and Lyft drivers, mandating just cause, advance notice, and appeal rights before firings. Passing with 40 votes in favor, 7 against, and 1 abstention, it sets the nation's strongest standard for gig worker protections, inspired by successful fast-food worker laws.<span class="inline-flex" data-state="closed">​</span></p>

"Together, we are standing united with workers and passing my legislation that will start a nationwide movement," Krishnan declared. Desai hailed it as ending the fear of waking up jobless after grueling shifts, empowering drivers who often go into debt for their vehicles.<span class="inline-flex" data-state="closed">​</span></p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">Many NYC drivers hail from Indian roots, making this victory especially resonant for the India-US diaspora powering the city's mobility. Organizations like NYTWA, led by Desai, have long championed these workers, building on decades of advocacy for fair wages and safety.
