<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">New York Governor Kathy Hochul signed groundbreaking legislation on December 26, 2025, requiring social media platforms to display prominent warning labels about potential mental health risks to young users. Platforms featuring infinite scrolling, auto-play videos, and algorithmic feeds must alert users upon initial interaction and periodically thereafter, ensuring no bypass option exists. This proactive measure targets addictive designs, drawing parallels to tobacco warnings for cancer risks or plastic packaging alerts for child safety.<span class="inline-flex" data-state="closed">​</span></p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">Hochul emphasized her commitment: "Keeping New Yorkers safe has been my top priority since taking office, and that includes protecting our kids from the potential harms of social media features that encourage excessive use." The law applies to activities occurring wholly or partly in New York, empowering the state attorney general to impose civil penalties up to $5,000 per violation. It aligns with national concerns, echoing the 2023 U.S. Surgeon General's advisory and joining states like California and Minnesota in youth protection efforts, while Australia recently banned social media for under-16s.<span class="inline-flex" data-state="closed">​</span></p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">This development resonates deeply, as families navigate similar digital challenges for their children thriving in New York's vibrant communities. Platforms like TikTok, Instagram, Snapchat, and YouTube will comply, fostering informed usage among tech-savvy youth of Indian heritage, balancing studies and cultural ties. Such policies promote healthier online habits, empowering parents to guide screen time effectively.<span class="inline-flex" data-state="closed">​</span></p>
