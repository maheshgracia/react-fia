f:T764,<p dir="ltr"><strong>Roundtable in New York highlights IIT Bombay alumni strength and emerging tech for India&rsquo;s future growth</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">The Indian Consulate in New York recently organized a high-level roundtable discussion titled &ldquo;Shaping India&rsquo;s Future Through Emerging Technologies&rdquo; in collaboration with the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay and the IIT Bombay Heritage Foundation. The event brought together prominent academics, diplomats, and industry leaders to explore how emerging technologies can drive India&rsquo;s growth and global standing.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">It also aimed to strengthen ties with the vast IIT Bombay alumni network in the United States. Dr. Shireesh Kedare, Director of IIT Bombay, delivered a key presentation on technology sovereignty and the vision of a developed &lsquo;Viksit Bharat.' Special remarks were made by Dr. Shih-Fu Chang, Dean of Columbia Engineering, and Binaya Srikanta Pradhan, Consul General of India in New York, who highlighted the growing technology partnership between India and the United States.</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>IIT Bombay Global Alumni Roundtable Strengthens Future Initiatives</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">Dr. Pratim Biswas, Dean of the College of Engineering at the University of Miami, praised the initiative on LinkedIn, calling it &ldquo;a great event&rdquo; and commending the efforts of the Indian Consulate, IIT Bombay, and the IIT Bombay Heritage Foundation.</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">Prof. Upendra Bhandarkar, Dean of Alumni and Corporate Relations at IIT Bombay, delivered the vote of thanks. The roundtable marked a significant step in leveraging the strength of IIT Bombay&rsquo;s global diaspora alumni to support the institute&rsquo;s future initiatives.</p>
