f:T71e,<h2 id="m_3586386498657634159executive-order-signals-pro-business-shift">Executive Order Signals Pro-Business Shift</h2>
<p>New York City Mayor Zohran Mamdani has signed Executive Order 11, launching a sweeping review of fees and fines that have long strained small businesses across the five boroughs. The move, inked at a local shop in Cypress Hills, Brooklyn, directs seven key agencies under Deputy Mayor for Economic Justice Julie Su to inventory over 6,000 regulations and propose cuts within tight deadlines. This initiative promises to ease the regulatory burden, fostering an environment where entrepreneurs can thrive amid rising operational costs.</p>
<p>Small businesses form the heartbeat of New York, generating jobs and infusing neighborhoods with vitality, yet they grapple with excessive penalties for minor infractions like missing refund signs or shelf prices. Mamdani emphasized this reality during the signing, stating the city must end its role in driving up expenses through outdated rules. Agency leaders now have 45 days to catalog reducible fees, 90 days to eliminate unnecessary ones via rulemaking, and 180 days to flag those needing legislative fixes, culminating in a year-long study on amnesty programs.</p>
<p>Chamber of Commerce representatives hailed the order as a promising step, though they urged quantifiable targets for fine reductions to ensure lasting impact. The Department of Small Business Services will also assess permit timelines, aiming to accelerate launches for new ventures. As New York navigates economic pressures, this pro-growth policy underscores a commitment to supporting the enterprises that define the city's resilient spirit.
