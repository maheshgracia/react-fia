f:T803,<p dir="ltr"><strong>Raj Goyle lands key endorsements from Sunrise Movement and NYCC, fueling a climate-focused comptroller bid</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">Just weeks after his arrest during a demonstration against Governor Kathy Hochul&rsquo;s proposed changes to New York&rsquo;s climate legislation, Raj Goyle, a candidate for state comptroller, has secured major endorsements from leading activist organizations. New York Communities for Change (NYCC) and the Sunrise Movement have thrown their weight behind his bid, boosting a campaign that puts aggressive climate action at its center.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Goyle has been outspoken on the campaign trail, slamming major energy companies like National Grid and Con Edison for hiking utility costs while &ldquo;burning down the planet.&rdquo; He promised to disrupt the status quo if elected, asserting the comptroller&rsquo;s office should serve as &ldquo;an engine for change&rdquo; rather than a barrier. At a recent rally, Goyle stood with members of the Sunrise Movement, known for championing the Green New Deal, and praised NYCC for its advocacy for affordable housing and progressive tax reform.</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Focus on Clean Energy and Working-Class Families</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">Goyle emphasized that residents in Buffalo, Brooklyn, and the Rockaways deserve clean air, manageable energy bills, and a healthy environment for future generations. He laid out a platform to divest state funds from fossil fuels and military spending, audit major corporations, and reject subsidies for high-energy AI data centers.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Instead, Goyle aims to channel investments into clean energy projects designed to deliver stable returns for pensioners while safeguarding the climate. Santosh Nandabalan, organizing director for NYCC, called Goyle a leader who prioritizes working-class families and immigrants. Kiana Ziadkhanpour of the Sunrise Movement echoed that sentiment, stating that New York needs a comptroller committed to a livable future for all.</p>
