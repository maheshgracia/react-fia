New York Indian Consulate Shines Amid Snowstorm for India's 77th Republic Day

Community Resilience and Young Achievers Honored Despite Harsh Weather

 Braving a massive snowstorm that blanketed the city and snarled highways, a dedicated crowd gathered at the Indian Consulate General here for a spirited celebration of India's 77th Republic Day on January 26. Consul General Binaya Srikanta Pradhan led the flag-hoisting ceremony and delivered President Droupadi Murmu's address, which underscored the Constitution's enduring values and the pivotal contributions of citizens at home and abroad toward a developed India by 2047.

The event highlighted the unbreakable spirit of the community, with attendees undeterred by inclement conditions to reaffirm ties with the homeland. In a special recognition, the consulate felicitated second-generation standouts Ronit Karki and Jiya Jadhav for their exceptional achievements across various fields, inspiring pride and excellence among attendees.​

India's Permanent Mission to the United Nations complemented the observance with a formal reception, fostering diplomatic goodwill in the heart of global affairs. These gatherings reflect the vibrant bridge between India and its partners in the U.S., embodying unity and progress even in adversity. As New York thaws from the storm, the message of constitutional strength resonates strongly.
