f:T730,<h2><em>Championing Excellence, Unity, and Community Service Among Indian American Physicians</em></h2>
<p>The Indian American Medical Association (IAMA) of Illinois celebrated its milestone 45th Annual Gala with grandeur and gratitude in Downers Grove, drawing over 200 physicians, guests, and community leaders for an evening that beautifully intertwined professional achievement, cultural heritage, and service to society. Emphasizing the growing influence of Indian Americans in the US healthcare landscape, the event spotlighted collaborative spirit, leadership, and impactful philanthropy.</p>
<p>The gala, hosted by Ashok and Megha Doshi and set to the inspiring notes of both the US and Indian national anthems, kicked off with reflections on the organization's achievements. Outgoing IAMA President Dr. Dilip Shah highlighted the chapter's commitment to service&mdash;providing free medical care to over 400 patients this year and raising more than $180,000 for charitable foundations. Notably, the organization emphasized nurturing the next generation through the Golden Stethoscope research awards and World Yoga Day collaborations.</p>
<p>Honors flowed throughout the evening. The newly elected executive team, including Dr. Rajeev Kumar (President), Dr. Neetha Dhananjaya (Secretary), and Dr. Vivek Mohan (Treasurer), received the community&rsquo;s vote of confidence. Recognitions such as the Distinguished Physician Award, the Woman Physician Leadership Award, and the President&rsquo;s Appreciation Award celebrated both medical excellence and dedicated community service. Local leadership was honored as well, demonstrating IAMA&rsquo;s strong civic ties.</p>
