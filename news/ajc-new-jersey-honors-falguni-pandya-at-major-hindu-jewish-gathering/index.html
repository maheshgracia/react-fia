AJC New Jersey Honors Falguni Pandya at Major Hindu-Jewish Gathering

Livingston, NJ - In a landmark celebration of interfaith solidarity, the American Jewish Committee (AJC) New Jersey presented its Interfaith Bridge-Building Leadership Award to Hindu American leader Falguni (Fal) Pandya on December 8, 2025. The event, co-hosted with the Hindu-Jewish Coalition at Temple Beth Shalom, drew over 200 attendees, marking one of the region's largest Hindu-Jewish gatherings.
 
AJC leaders hailed Pandya as a pivotal voice strengthening Hindu-Jewish relations. In her acceptance, Pandya described the honor as “a meeting of my two worlds,” emphasizing that her bridge-building work long preceded recent heightened attention.
 
She stressed the strategic importance of unity: “Together, we are 13 million people. We can protect ourselves when we protect each other.” Pandya referenced the millennia-old legacy of Hindu protection of Jewish refugees and introduced “Tikkun Vishwa,” a concept blending Hebrew and Sanskrit terms for “healing the world.”
 
The event featured a dialogue on 2,000 years of Hindu-Jewish ties and was attended by a broad coalition of Jewish, Hindu, interfaith, and civic institutions, signaling a new era of partnership in New Jersey.

Fal (Falguni) Pandya is a Hindu American interfaith leader and a leading national voice in Hindu-Jewish relations. She serves on the boards of the Hindu-Jewish Coalition and India-Israel Coalition, and has been appointed by Governor Phil Murphy to the NJ-India Commission and the AAPI Commission.
