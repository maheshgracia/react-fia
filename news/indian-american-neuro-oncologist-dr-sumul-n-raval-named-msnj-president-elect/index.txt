f:T720,<p dir="ltr"><strong>Renowned neurologist to lead America's oldest medical society, focusing on physician burnout and policy reform</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">The Medical Society of New Jersey (MSNJ) has officially named Indian-American neurologist Dr. Sumul N. Raval as its president-elect, marking a historic moment for the nation&rsquo;s oldest medical organization. Elected with strong backing from fellow physicians, Dr. Raval&rsquo;s new role comes at a time when the healthcare sector is grappling with high levels of burnout, complex policy shifts, and the ongoing struggle for better patient access.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Dr. Raval is a heavyweight in the neurological field, having established New Jersey&rsquo;s very first comprehensive brain tumor center at Monmouth Medical Center. As a board-certified neuro-oncologist, he has spent years at the intersection of clinical innovation and compassionate care. Now, he&rsquo;s shifting his focus toward broader systemic change.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Dr. Raval to Lead MSNJ with Focus on Reform and Physician Well-Being</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">His upcoming leadership is expected to prioritize the mental well-being of healthcare workers while pushing for legislative reforms, such as cutting through the "red tape" of insurance pre-authorizations that often delay critical treatments. Established in 1766, well before the American Medical Association, MSNJ represents over 30,000 doctors.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">Dr. Raval&rsquo;s appointment ensures that the society remains a fierce advocate for the sacred physician-patient bond. By bridging his deep clinical experience with a forward-thinking vision for medical advocacy, he aims to guide the Garden State&rsquo;s medical community through the evolving pressures of modern practice.</p>
