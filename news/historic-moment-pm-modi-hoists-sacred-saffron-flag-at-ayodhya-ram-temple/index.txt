f:T65e,<div><em>Dhwajarohan Ceremony Marks Spiritual Completion of Ram Mandir, Strengthening Cultural Pride for Indian Diaspora</em></div>
<div>
<p>Prime Minister Narendra Modi ceremoniously hoisted a sacred saffron flag atop the 191-foot shikhar of the Ayodhya Ram Temple on November 25, 2025, marking the symbolic completion of this monumental spiritual project. The flag-hoisting event, known as "Dhwajarohan," took place on the auspicious day of Vivah Panchami, which celebrates the divine marriage of Lord Ram and Goddess Sita. This historic moment was witnessed by thousands of devotees and dignitaries, including RSS Chief Mohan Bhagwat, Uttar Pradesh Governor Anandiben Patel, and Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath.</p>
<p>The specially crafted saffron flag is 22 feet long and 11 feet wide, made following Defence Ministry specifications by a parachute expert from Ahmedabad. It bears significant symbols such as a radiant Sun, the Om symbol, and the Kovidara tree, representing Lord Ram&rsquo;s Suryavanshi heritage and spiritual sacrifice. The flag will fly atop the temple as a symbol of pride, heritage, and the triumph of truth over falsehood, echoing the sentiments expressed by PM Modi during his address.</p>
<p>The event marks the formal completion of the Ram Mandir complex, a project that began after the Supreme Court's landmark 2019 verdict and the foundation stone laid by PM Modi in August 2020, amidst the COVID-19 pandemic. The ceremony&rsquo;s live telecast was made accessible across Ayodhya through more than 50 LED screens, ensuring widespread participation despite seating limits at the venue.</p>
