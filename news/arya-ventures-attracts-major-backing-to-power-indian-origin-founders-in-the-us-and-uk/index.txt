f:T853,<p><em>New INR 4.2 crore commitment from billionaire entrepreneur Siddharth Shankar strengthens a $10 million fund focused on post-revenue startups.</em></p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">Arya Ventures, a seed-stage venture capital fund dedicated to Indian-origin founders, has secured an INR 4.2 crore (about $500,000) commitment from billionaire entrepreneur Siddharth Shankar, Global COO of Komerz. This investment is a key milestone for the INR 84 crore ($10 million) fund, which is designed to back post-product, post-revenue startups founded by Indian diaspora entrepreneurs in the US and UK.​</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">The fund&rsquo;s thesis is anchored in strong data: Indian immigrants have founded or co-founded 72 of 358 American unicorns since 2018, representing roughly 19% of these high-growth companies despite being just over 2% of the US population. In the UK, Indian diaspora-owned companies generate an estimated INR 3.87 lakh crore ($46 billion) in revenues and employ more than 174,000 people, underscoring the diaspora&rsquo;s economic weight.​</p>
<h2 id="portfolio-traction-and-focus-sectors" class="mb-2 mt-4 font-display font-semimedium text-base first:mt-0 md:text-lg [hr+&amp;]:mt-4">Portfolio traction and focus sectors</h2>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">Arya Ventures has already invested in startups such as Avi Vehicles, KnowYourDosh, and Prescribe Life, which are reporting sharp valuation uplifts, revenue growth, and successful movement toward seed rounds. The fund typically writes initial cheques of INR 1.68&ndash;2.1 crore ($200,000&ndash;$250,000), with scope for follow-on investments, and is heavily focused on technology and AI-driven ventures in its pipeline, including companies linked to global accelerators like Y Combinator.
