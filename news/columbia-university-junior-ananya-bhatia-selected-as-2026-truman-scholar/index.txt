f:T6b2,<p dir="ltr"><strong id="docs-internal-guid-9d707133-7fff-b3aa-6842-7b9cc1a5cbbd">Indian American student Ananya Bhatia earns prestigious Truman Scholarship for leadership in international law</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">Columbia University junior Ananya Bhatia has been named a 2026 Truman Scholar, marking a significant milestone in her journey as a public service leader.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">Selected from a highly competitive pool of nearly 800 applicants, Bhatia is one of 55 students nationwide to receive the honor, which recognizes undergraduates committed to careers in government and advocacy.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Majoring in Political Science and Urban Studies, Bhatia&rsquo;s academic focus centers on the complexities of ethnic conflict and the prosecution of war crimes. Her dedication to justice is not merely theoretical; she has actively served as a grassroots advocate and interned with the Orleans Public Defenders Office and Reprieve.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Truman Scholar Bhatia Pursues Career in Human Rights Law</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">Through these roles, she has tackled critical issues ranging from capital punishment to the impact of conflict on civilian populations. Bhatia, who hails from Shreveport, Louisiana, has also been a vocal proponent of menstrual equity and youth justice reform in her home state.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">At Columbia, she contributes to the Undergraduate Law Review and the Eric H. Holder Jr. Initiative for Civil and Political Rights. With the support of the Truman Scholarship, she plans to pursue a JD with a focus on international criminal law, aiming to build a career dedicated to global social justice and human rights.</p>
