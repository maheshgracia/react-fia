f:T8b8,<div><strong>Jaskaran Singh&rsquo;s heartfelt coming-of-age drama &lsquo;Jersey Boy&rsquo; makes its world premiere at CAAMFest 2026</strong></div>
<p dir="ltr">Jaskaran Singh&rsquo;s feature directorial debut, &lsquo;Jersey Boy,' will have its world premiere on May 9 at 7 p.m. at the AMC Kabuki theater in San Francisco&rsquo;s Japantown as part of CAAMFest 2026, running May 7-10. The 105-minute coming-of-age drama follows Karandeep Singh through three life stages: childhood, teenage years, and adulthood against a post-9/11 backdrop.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">It explores themes of identity, belonging, family bonds, and personal loss as the young protagonist grapples with relationships and tragedy. The central role is played by three actors: Rayaan Singh Sahni as a child, Surjun Lail as a teenager, and Praneet Akilla as an adult. Sara Garcia rounds out the cast. Shot in English and Punjabi, the film was produced in Canada.</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>From Script to Screen: A Personal Milestone&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">Singh, who was born in Brampton and raised in New Jersey, has built a career around stories of identity and inclusion through earlier short films such as the docu-drama &lsquo;A TURBAN &amp; A BEARD&rsquo; on post-9/11 Sikh hate crimes, the romantic dramedy &lsquo;ME|HER&rsquo;, and the psychological drama &lsquo;COPELESS&rsquo;.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">Looking back on the long journey, Singh said, &ldquo;It&rsquo;s still unreal that something I started writing in 2019 and we filmed in 2023 is finally seeing the light of day&hellip; it&rsquo;s lit up my world in the darkest of days, and I&rsquo;m beyond excited to share its shine. It&rsquo;s time to come home.&rdquo;&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">He thanked festival organizers with a simple &ldquo;Thanks.&rdquo; The screenplay earned recognition earlier, reaching the quarterfinals of the 2022 Academy Nicholl Fellowships and landing on the 2021 CAPE List curated by The Black List. It also won the Power Pitch award at the 2020 Whistler Film Festival. Now in its 44th year, CAAMFest remains one of the premier platforms for Asian American and Pacific Islander cinema, spotlighting fresh and diverse storytelling voices.</p>
