f:T6f6,<p dir="ltr"><strong>Bethell hits back at Alastair Cook, claiming IPL bench time offers more growth than returning to county cricket</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">England&rsquo;s rising star Jacob Bethell has issued a firm rebuttal to former captain Sir Alastair Cook, defending his decision to remain with Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) despite a lack of playing time.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">The debate ignited after Cook suggested on the Stick to Cricket podcast that the 22-year-old was essentially "sitting on his a***" in India. Cook argued that Bethell would be better served returning home to open for Warwickshire in the County Championship to prepare for England&rsquo;s future assignments.</p>
<p dir="ltr">However, Bethell remained unfazed, emphasizing that development isn't solely about minutes on the field. Speaking on the Sky Sports Cricket Podcast, the all-rounder noted that being immersed in the high-pressure environment of the IPL, where even practice sessions are scrutinized by "hundreds of eyes," offers a unique form of growth.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Bethell Embraces IPL Pressure as Path to Growth</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">"I feel better now than I was a month ago," Bethell remarked, highlighting that there is no "right or wrong way" to evolve as a cricketer. The young star further explained that the sheer standard of the tournament forced players to subconsciously "up their game."&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">His stance found a vocal ally in Kevin Pietersen, who blasted Cook on social media, claiming the former opener has "no idea" what the IPL experience provides. For Bethell, the "intangible benefits" of training alongside world-class legends outweigh the traditional route of early-season domestic red-ball matches.</p>
