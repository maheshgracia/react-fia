Murali’s Wrist, Ashwin’s Mind: The Rise of Jarren Bacher at CSK

Ali Bacher’s grandson brings mystery spin to the IPL, blending legendary tactics with hypermobile talent

In the high-octane environment of the Chennai Super Kings (CSK) training camp for IPL 2026, a new face has been turning heads, not just for his famous lineage but for a bowling action that feels like a blast from the past. Jarren Bacher, the 22-year-old grandson of South African icon Ali Bacher, has officially joined the five-time champions as a net bowler, bringing a unique brand of "mystery spin" to the Chepauk nets.

What makes Bacher a nightmare for batters like Ruturaj Gaikwad and Sarfaraz Khan is his hypermobility. Much like the legendary Muttiah Muralitharan, Bacher possesses exceptionally flexible joints that allow him to whip the ball from the back of his hand. This physiological advantage enables him to fire in a "doosra" with a remarkably straight arm, a delivery that most modern off-spinners struggle to execute legally.

Bacher Channels Ravichandran Ashwin’s Mindset in Spin Craft

"I release it from the back of my hand," Bacher explained during a recent session at Guru Nanak College. "My stock ball can deviate both ways depending on the hitter’s position. I’m more of a 'quick' spinner than a traditional flight-and-guile bowler." While his mechanics mirror Murali, his mental blueprint is purely Ravichandran Ashwin. 

Bacher has openly admitted that Ashwin has been his idol since the age of 13. He isn't just a fan of the Indian veteran's carrom ball; he is obsessed with his psychological warfare. "Ashwin is a warrior. He never backs down," Bacher noted. "I love that batters fear him like they would a strike seamer. That aggressive mindset is what I try to bring to every ball."

Bacher’s journey to Chennai wasn't direct. After a stint with the Joburg Super Kings in SA20, where he picked the brain of Imran Tahir, he was invited to the CSK camp to test the best in the business. Working under CSK spin coach S. Sriram, Bacher is currently refining his tactical angles. Whether this net-bowling stint leads to a full IPL contract remains to be seen, but for now, the young South African is successfully blending Sri Lankan mystery with Indian tenacity.
