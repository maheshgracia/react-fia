f:T764,<p dir="ltr"><strong>Community members engage lawmakers, raise concerns on Hinduphobia, and push for policy recognition</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">In a significant step toward greater civic engagement, Minnesota hosted its first-ever Hindu Advocacy Day on April 16 at the State Capitol. Organized by the Coalition of Hindus of North America (CoHNA), the event brought together community members to discuss key issues facing Hindu Americans.</p>
<p dir="ltr">More than two dozen volunteers, including a strong showing of young people and families, participated in meetings with legislators and staff. Despite the busy legislative session limiting lawmaker attendance, the conversations were described as thoughtful and constructive.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Participants highlighted growing concerns over Hinduphobia, rising incidents of temple vandalism, and anti-Hindu hate crimes in the United States. Global issues, particularly the situation of Hindus in Bangladesh, were also discussed.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Minnesota Hindus Unite for Recognition and Representation</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">The diverse Hindu community in Minnesota, estimated at 40,000 - 45,000 and including people of Indian, Guyanese, Nepali, Sri Lankan, and other origins, came together under the shared values of Sanatan Dharma.</p>
<p dir="ltr">The event builds on recent grassroots efforts supporting Senate File 4115, a resolution aimed at recognizing anti-Hindu discrimination as a human rights concern. Organizers described the day as part of a long-term strategy to increase representation and build stronger relationships with policymakers.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">Traditional Indian snacks added a warm cultural touch to the gathering. This inaugural Advocacy Day reflects a broader national trend of Hindu Americans becoming more actively involved in public policy and civic life.</p>
