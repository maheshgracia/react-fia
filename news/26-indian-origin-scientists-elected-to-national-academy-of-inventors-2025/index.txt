f:Ta20,<h2 class="mb-2 mt-4 font-display font-semimedium text-base first:mt-0">Prestigious Recognition Highlights Innovation in US Academia</h2>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">Twenty-six scientists of Indian origin have been named to the 2025 class of Fellows by the National Academy of Inventors (NAI), celebrating their groundbreaking contributions to technology, health, and engineering that enhance lives worldwide. This elite honor, the highest for academic inventors, recognizes those whose inventions drive economic growth and societal progress, with the new class holding over 5,300 US patents across 127 institutions.<span class="inline-flex" data-state="closed">​</span></p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">Among the standout achievers is Raghuraman Kannan, Curators&rsquo; Distinguished Professor at the University of Missouri-Columbia, renowned for cancer research innovations like targeted nanomedicine therapies. Kripa K. Varanasi, professor of mechanical engineering at MIT, earns acclaim for surface engineering breakthroughs that boost energy efficiency and food safety. Other luminaries include Anant Agarwal from The Ohio State University, advancing materials science; Rohit Bhargava at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, pioneering bio-spectroscopy; and Satyandra Gupta at USC, revolutionizing robotics and manufacturing.<span class="inline-flex" aria-label="Raghuraman Kannan named 2025 NAI Fellow| India Abroad" data-state="closed">​</span><span class="inline-flex" data-state="closed">​</span></p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">This cohort also features Deepakraj Divan (Georgia Tech), Swaroop Ghosh (Penn State), Dipanjan Pan (Penn State), Shashank Priya (University of Minnesota), and Kripa Varanasi, underscoring Indian talent's pivotal role in US innovation hubs. Their work spans cancer-fighting nanoparticles, sustainable energy solutions, and AI-driven manufacturing, promising global benefits.<span class="inline-flex" data-state="closed">​</span></p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">The Fellows will be inducted at the NAI's 15th Annual Conference on June 4, 2026, in Los Angeles, inspiring the Indian diaspora to pursue excellence in STEM.
