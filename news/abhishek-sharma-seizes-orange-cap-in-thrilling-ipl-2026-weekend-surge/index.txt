f:T643,<p dir="ltr"><strong>Abhishek edges out teenage star Sooryavanshi and KL Rahul as the lead changes four times in a single weekend</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">The IPL 2026 Orange Cap race has turned into a high-speed game of musical chairs, with the leaderboard seeing four different occupants in a single weekend. What started as a steady season for the veterans has quickly exploded into a showcase of raw, youthful power hitting.</p>
<p dir="ltr">The veteran Virat Kohli held his ground early on, but the momentum shifted during the Delhi Capitals vs Punjab Kings clash. KL Rahul made a massive statement by becoming the first Indian batter to cross the 150-run mark in an IPL innings, smashing a record-breaking 152 to briefly snatch the lead.</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Abhishek Sharma Reclaims Top Spot After Rahul and Sooryavanshi Shake-Up&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">However, Rahul's tenure at the top lasted only hours. Rajasthan Royals&rsquo; 15-year-old sensation Vaibhav Sooryavanshi set the Sawai Mansingh Stadium on fire with a 36-ball century against Sunrisers Hyderabad. His blistering 103 leveled him with Rahul at 357 runs, but he took the top spot thanks to a superior strike rate.</p>
<p dir="ltr">The drama didn't end there. In the very same match, SRH&rsquo;s Abhishek Sharma reclaimed the crown during the chase. His brisk 57 off 29 balls propelled him to 380 runs for the season, effectively snatching the Orange Cap back from the teenager. As of April 27, Abhishek sits at the summit, leading a pack where the top five are separated by fewer than 60 runs.</p>
