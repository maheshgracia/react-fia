f:T842,<p dir="ltr"><strong>The city will host free screenings, fan zones, and cultural events across all five boroughs during the tournament</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">In a major step toward building excitement for the FIFA World Cup 2026, New York City officials have announced a lineup of free fan events set to take place across all five boroughs beginning this June. The initiative, led by Mayor Zohran Mamdani in partnership with the FIFA World Cup 2026 New York/New Jersey Host Committee, aims to make the tournament accessible to residents and visitors alike.</p>
<p dir="ltr">The events will feature live match screenings, cultural performances, and interactive experiences designed to capture the spirit of the global competition. Speaking at a public gathering at Staten Island University Hospital Community Park on April 27, the mayor emphasized the importance of shared experiences beyond the stadium.</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>NYC Gears Up as 2026 World Cup Hub with Multi-Borough Fan Zones</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">The 2026 edition of the World Cup, co-hosted by the United States, Canada, and Mexico, will be the largest in history, featuring 48 teams and 104 matches across 16 cities. Key fixtures, including the final, are scheduled to take place at MetLife Stadium, while the opening match will be held at Estadio Azteca.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Across New York City, designated fan zones will bring the action closer to communities. Venues include the Billie Jean King National Tennis Center in Queens, Rockefeller Center in Manhattan, Bronx Terminal Market, Brooklyn Bridge Park, and Staten Island&rsquo;s community park.</p>
<p dir="ltr">New York Governor Kathy Hochul highlighted the inclusive nature of the initiative, noting that it would unite communities while supporting local businesses. Organizers also confirmed infrastructure upgrades, including a new LED scoreboard at the Staten Island venue, ensuring long-term benefits beyond the tournament. With preparations gaining momentum, the city is positioning itself as a vibrant hub for fans during the historic global event.</p>
