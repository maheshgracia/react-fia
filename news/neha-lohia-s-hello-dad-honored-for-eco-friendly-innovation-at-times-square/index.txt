f:T735,<p dir="ltr"><strong>Indian American filmmaker Neha Lohia earns acclaim for solar-powered production during Earth Day celebrations</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">Amid the neon lights of Times Square, Indian American filmmaker Neha Lohia took center stage during recent Earth Day festivities to showcase a different kind of brilliance.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">Her short film, Hello Dad, was officially recognized for its pioneering commitment to sustainable filmmaking, proving that cinematic excellence does not have to come at an environmental cost.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Produced with a minimal carbon footprint, the project utilized solar-powered equipment and resource-efficient techniques, earning the prestigious Environmental Media Association&rsquo;s Green Seal.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Lohia&rsquo;s Film Explores Grief, Connection, and Human Resilience</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">Lohia, joined by lead actors Tirlok Malik and Ankita Makwana, reflected on her roots, noting that the Indian concept of "Dharti Maa" (Mother Earth) inspires her to remain as "weightless" as possible during production.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Beyond its technical achievements, the film is a deeply personal exploration of grief and human connection. Lohia urged the audience to cherish their loved ones, noting that the project helped her navigate her own journey of loss.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">The event, organized by the Good News Corporation, featured tributes to the Jane Goodall Foundation and saw participation from global icons like Oprah Winfrey and Leonardo DiCaprio.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">As Hello Dad continues its run on the international festival circuit, it stands as a testament to a new era where the medium and the message are equally green.</p>
