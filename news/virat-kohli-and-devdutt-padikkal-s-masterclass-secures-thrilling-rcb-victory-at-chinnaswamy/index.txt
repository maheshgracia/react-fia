<p dir="ltr">The M. Chinnaswamy Stadium erupted on Friday night as Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) pulled off a spectacular five-wicket victory over the Gujarat Titans (GT), further cementing their status as the league's most formidable chasing unit.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">Chasing a daunting 206, the home side rode on a vintage masterclass from Virat Kohli and a blistering cameo from Devdutt Padikkal to cross the line with seven balls to spare.</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Kohli and Padikkal Power Bengaluru&rsquo;s Commanding Chase</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">The chase didn't start smoothly for Bengaluru, with early wickets creating a ripple of tension in the stands. However, the momentum shifted once Kohli and Padikkal united at the crease.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">Kohli, who finished with an unbeaten 81 off just 44 deliveries, anchored the innings with surgical precision while reaching historic milestones: 800 IPL fours and 300 sixes for a single franchise.</p>
<p dir="ltr">His partner in crime, Padikkal, was the primary aggressor, smashing a sensational 20-ball half-century. His final tally of 55 runs from 27 balls effectively dismantled the Titans' bowling rhythm.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">Though both set batters fell in quick succession, Krunal Pandya&rsquo;s late-over heroics ensured the win was sealed in the 19th over. This victory marks RCB&rsquo;s 50th win at the Chinnaswamy, joining an elite group of teams with half-a-century of home wins at a single venue.</p>
