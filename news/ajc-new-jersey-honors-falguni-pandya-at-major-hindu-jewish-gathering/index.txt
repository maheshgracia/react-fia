f:T6bd,<div dir="auto">Livingston, NJ - In a landmark celebration of interfaith solidarity, the American Jewish Committee (AJC) New Jersey presented its Interfaith Bridge-Building Leadership Award to Hindu American leader Falguni (Fal) Pandya on December 8, 2025. The event, co-hosted with the Hindu-Jewish Coalition at Temple Beth Shalom, drew over 200 attendees, marking one of the region's largest Hindu-Jewish gatherings.</div>
<div dir="auto">&nbsp;</div>
<div dir="auto">AJC leaders hailed Pandya as a pivotal voice strengthening Hindu-Jewish relations. In her acceptance, Pandya described the honor as &ldquo;a meeting of my two worlds,&rdquo; emphasizing that her bridge-building work long preceded recent heightened attention.</div>
<div dir="auto">&nbsp;</div>
<div dir="auto">She stressed the strategic importance of unity: &ldquo;Together, we are 13 million people. We can protect ourselves when we protect each other.&rdquo; Pandya referenced the millennia-old legacy of Hindu protection of Jewish refugees and introduced &ldquo;Tikkun Vishwa,&rdquo; a concept blending Hebrew and Sanskrit terms for &ldquo;healing the world.&rdquo;</div>
<div dir="auto">&nbsp;</div>
<div dir="auto">The event featured a dialogue on 2,000 years of Hindu-Jewish ties and was attended by a broad coalition of Jewish, Hindu, interfaith, and civic institutions, signaling a new era of partnership in New Jersey.</div>
<p dir="auto">Fal (Falguni) Pandya is a Hindu American interfaith leader and a leading national voice in Hindu-Jewish relations.
