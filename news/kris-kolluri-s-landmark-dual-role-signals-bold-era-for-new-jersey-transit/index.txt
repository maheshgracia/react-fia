f:T738,<p><em>Proven Leader from Rutgers to Lead State's Top Agencies Ahead of World Cup Surge</em></p>
<p>Governor-elect Mikie Sherrill has selected Kris Kolluri, the current president and CEO of NJ TRANSIT, to also helm the New Jersey Turnpike Authority as executive director, a strategic move to synchronize the state's premier transit and highway operations. This rare dual appointment positions Kolluri to break down silos, streamline commutes, and gear up for the 2026 FIFA World Cup, when tens of thousands of visitors will flood the region. Kolluri will draw a nominal $1 salary from the Turnpike Authority while retaining his $280,000 NJ TRANSIT post, underscoring a commitment to unified efficiency.</p>
<p>Kolluri, born in Hyderabad, India, and a Rutgers University alumnus with a law degree from Georgetown, brings unmatched expertise to the role. His career highlights include steering the $16.1 billion Hudson Tunnel Project as CEO of the Gateway Development Commission, securing record federal funding and launching construction on new rail tunnels beneath the Hudson River. Earlier, he served as New Jersey transportation commissioner from 2006 to 2008, chaired boards for NJ TRANSIT and the Turnpike Authority, and advised top Democrats on Capitol Hill, including as senior policy advisor to former House Leader Richard Gephardt on India-related matters.</p>
<p>Sherrill praised Kolluri's "decades of experience with every major transit agency in the region," noting his leadership will ensure safer, smoother travel for residents and guests alike. NJ Department of Transportation Commissioner Fran O'Connor echoed this, highlighting Kolluri's readiness for World Cup demands through real-time decision-making.
