f:T60d,<h2 dir="ltr">Kohli becomes the first player to hit the 9k mark in the history of the IPL</h2>
<p dir="ltr">The 2026 edition of the Indian Premier League (IPL) continues to deliver historic moments. In a high-stakes encounter at the Arun Jaitley Stadium on Monday night, Virat Kohli etched his name deeper into the history books, becoming the first player in IPL history to surpass the 9,000-run mark in IPL.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Needing just 11 runs to reach the milestone as he stepped out for the chase, the "King" wasted no time in front of his emotional home crowd. Kohli achieved the feat on the final ball of the powerplay, driving Axar Patel to long-off for a single, triggering a massive roar from the stands. He finished the game unbeaten on 23 off 15 balls, fittingly sealing the win with two consecutive sixes.</p>
<h3 dir="ltr">RCB&rsquo;s Ruthless Performance</h3>
<p dir="ltr">While Kohli grabbed the headlines, the victory was set up by a historic bowling performance. RCB&rsquo;s pace duo of Josh Hazlewood (4/12) and Bhuvneshwar Kumar (3/5) dismantled the Capitals' top order, leaving the hosts reeling at 8/6 at one stage. DC were eventually bundled out for a mere 75, a target RCB chased down in just 6.3 overs.</p>
<p dir="ltr">The win catapults RCB to second on the points table with six victories from eight matches. For the Bengaluru faithful, while the 9,000-run milestone is a moment to savor, the focus remains firmly on the clinical form their talisman is carrying into the business end of the season.</p>
