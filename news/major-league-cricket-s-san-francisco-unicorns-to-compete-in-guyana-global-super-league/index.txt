f:T67c,<p dir="ltr"><strong>U.S. franchise joins elite T20 giants for a high-stakes international tournament in Providence this July</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">The San Francisco Unicorns have officially joined the ExxonMobil Guyana Global Super League (GSL), marking a pivotal moment for American cricket on the international stage. Scheduled to take place from July 23 to August 1, 2026, at the Guyana National Stadium in Providence, the tournament will see the Major League Cricket (MLC) franchise compete against elite T20 teams from across the globe.</p>
<p dir="ltr">GSL Chairman Clive Lloyd emphasized that the Unicorns' participation underscores the burgeoning influence of the United States in the cricketing world. The tournament features a prestigious lineup, including the Guyana Amazon Warriors, Lahore Qalandars, Desert Vipers, and a Perth Scorchers XI.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Unicorns Set for Global Test in Guyana T20 Showdown</strong></p>
<p dir="ltr">For the Unicorns, a founding MLC team supported by Cricket Victoria, this provides a high-stakes platform to test their skills against seasoned international veterans. Unicorns CEO David White expressed enthusiasm for the move, noting that the opportunity to play in Guyana&rsquo;s passionate cricketing environment is invaluable for his squad.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">By bridging the gap between emerging markets like the U.S. and established cricket hubs, the GSL aims to solidify its reputation as a premier T20 showcase. As the ten-day competition culminates in the final on August 1, all eyes will be on how the American side fares in this cross-continental clash.</p>
